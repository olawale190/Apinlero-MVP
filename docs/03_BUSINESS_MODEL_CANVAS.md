## Executive Summary

This document outlines the business model for **Àpínlẹ̀rọ**, an AI-powered operations platform serving UK SMEs across 10 high-value sectors. The model is based on a **horizontal SaaS platform** approach with industry-specific templates, tiered pricing (£150-£350/month), and a community-first go-to-market strategy.

**Platform Tagline:** *"One Platform. Any Channel. Any Business."*

**Key Business Metrics (Year 1 Targets):**
- **Customers:** 45 paying customers
- **MRR:** £13,250/month
- **ARPU:** £294/month
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** 23:1
- **Gross Margin:** ~75%

## 1. Customer Segments

**Target Market: UK SMEs Across 10 High-Value Sectors**

### Primary Segments

**Segment 1: Field Services**
- Industries: HVAC, Plumbing, Electricians
- Size: 5-50 employees
- Revenue: £100K-£2M
- Pain Point: Service requests via phone/WhatsApp/website — no centralized view
- ARPU: £300-350/month

**Segment 2: Client/Appointment Services**
- Industries: Dental, Healthcare, Real Estate
- Size: 5-30 employees
- Revenue: £200K-£2M
- Pain Point: Manual appointment scheduling, high no-shows
- ARPU: £300-350/month

**Segment 3: Operations/Logistics**
- Industries: Security, Cleaning, Logistics
- Size: 10-50 employees
- Revenue: £150K-£2M
- Pain Point: WhatsApp/email coordination for shift assignments
- ARPU: £250-300/month
- **Letters of Intent:** Security Services, Cleaning Services

**Segment 4: E-commerce/Retail**
- Industries: Specialty Grocery, Personal Services
- Size: 3-20 employees
- Revenue: £100K-£500K
- Pain Point: Orders across physical store, online, WhatsApp, phone
- ARPU: £250-300/month
- **Pilot Customer:** Isha's Treat & Groceries

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

| Characteristic | Specification |
|---------------|---------------|
| Size | 5-50 employees |
| Revenue | £100K-£2M annual |
| Channels | 3+ channels (WhatsApp, phone, website, walk-in) |
| Pain Point | 10+ hours/week on manual admin |
| Tech Adoption | Low-to-moderate |
| Decision Maker | Business owner or operations manager |
| Geographic | UK (Year 1: London, Birmingham, Manchester) |

### Market Sizing

| Market | Size | Description |
|--------|------|-------------|
| TAM | 5.5 million | All UK SMEs |
| SAM | 500,000 | SMEs with 5-50 employees in service/retail |
| SOM (Year 3) | 400 | 0.08% of SAM |

## 2. Value Propositions

**Core Value:** *Enterprise-level multi-channel operations + AI-powered insights at SME-affordable pricing (£150-£350/month)*

### Key Differentiators

| vs. Competitor | Àpínlẹ̀rọ Advantage |
|---------------|-------------------|
| **Shopify** | All channels including offline (46% UK SMEs have no website) |
| **WhatsApp Business** | Multi-channel + AI + automation (not just WhatsApp) |
| **HubSpot/Salesforce** | £250-350/month vs £1,000+ (SME-affordable) |
| **Zapier/Make** | Pre-configured, zero tech needed |
| **Generic AI Tools** | SME Knowledge Graph, sector-specific models |

### Proprietary Technology (Competitive Moat)

**1. SME Operations Knowledge Graph (Neo4j):**
- 47 entity types, 89 relationship types
- Data flywheel: Improves for ALL customers
- 3+ years to replicate

**2. Custom ML Models:**
- Demand Predictor (forecasts + cultural events)
- Anomaly Detector (learns unique SME patterns)
- Classification Engine (auto-categorizes by urgency)

**3. Voice AI (Year 2):**
- 24/7 voice ordering and appointment booking
- Vapi integration with Knowledge Graph context

### Value by Segment

**Field Services:**
- Centralize service requests (phone, WhatsApp, website)
- AI prioritization: "3 urgent jobs detected"
- Voice AI emergency callouts (Year 2)
- Save 8-12 hours/week

**Client/Appointment:**
- 24/7 Voice AI appointment booking (Year 2)
- Reduce no-shows 15% → 5%
- Prevent double bookings
- Free up staff time

**Operations/Logistics:**
- Unified shift assignments and client requests
- Automated customer notifications
- Incident reporting and tracking
- Reduce communication errors

**E-commerce/Retail:**
- Capture all orders from all channels
- AI Daily Insights: "Rice 5kg running low - restock now"
- Demand forecasting
- Recover 5-8 lost orders/week

## 5. Revenue Streams

### Pricing Structure

| Tier | Price | Target | Features | Launch |
|------|-------|--------|----------|--------|
| Free Trial | £0 (30 days) | All prospects | Full features, limited transactions | Year 1 |
| Solo | £150/month | Solopreneurs | Core features | Q1 2026 |
| Starter | £250/month | 5-15 employees | All channels, email support | Year 1 |
| Growth | £350/month | 15-30 employees | All features, priority support | Year 1 |

### Strategic Tier Rollout

**Year 1 Focus:** Starter (£250) + Growth (£350)
- Validate product-market fit with established SMEs
- Build compelling case studies

**Solo Tier (£150) Launch Q1 Year 2:**
- Why not Year 1? Solopreneurs = same onboarding, <50% revenue
- Upgrade path: 20% upgrade to Starter within 6 months

### Unit Economics

| Metric | Value | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| ARPU | £294/month | Weighted avg (Starter £250, Growth £350) |
| CAC | £150 | Community-based vs industry £300-500 |
| LTV | £3,528 | ARPU £294 × 12 months |
| **LTV:CAC** | **23:1** | Conservative, accounts for churn |
| Gross Margin | ~75% | Low infrastructure costs |
| Churn | 5%/month | Target (industry: 5-7%) |
| Payback Period | 0.5 months | CAC £150 ÷ £220 margin = 0.5 months |

### Willingness to Pay Evidence

- **Pilot:** Isha's Treat indicated £400/month willingness
- **Market Comparisons:**
  - Xero: £36-84/month
  - Square: £60-300/month
  - Shopify Plus: £250-2,000/month
- **ROI:** Time saved (£150/month) + revenue recovered (£25/month) = £175 value
